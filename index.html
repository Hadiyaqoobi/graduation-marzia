Happy Graduation

To my Little Graduated sister.

DEAR my beloved SISTER

Before starting, I want to say that I am so proud of you. I am so proud of everything you have accomplished; I am proud of you for overcoming the obstacles in your path and proving to so many people that you can achieve anything you set your mind to.

Dear little sister, the journey you have been on these past 12 years has not been easy. Indeed it hasn't been easy for all of us, after what happened to father we went through so many problems, mom, Homayoon and I, we were all busy dealing with the problems which we had, that's why we couldn't support you and be there for you when you needed us. And yet, as youngest member of our family, you coped even better with everything and found the courage and resilience that kept you moving forward.

Without asking our help, and without any support from us, you were the top of your school and now you are graduating as a top student from the university and as much as we would like to take credit for our part in getting you to where you are today, the fact is, it was your that strength, spirit, tenacity, grit and that courage that played the greatest role in all that you have achieved. You are the strongest, brightest, and the most passionate person I ever known.

Today signals the completion of one chapter of your life, but also the start of a fresh one. The pages are still blank and waiting for you to write your own story. I have no doubt you will fulfill it with the warmest of characters, the most beautiful of songs, stories of meaning, messages of hope, and that each word will be filled with passion and success. And even when the pages have times of difficulty, which is certain to occur, I know you will dig down deep and tap into those very same assets that have helped you before, and in the end, you will persevere and rise even more potent for the difficulties.

I have faith in you, not just me we all have faith in you we always had, and we will always have. We believe in your ability and your dreams. We believe in your desires and passions. We believe in your strengths, your potential, and all of the commitment that resides within. And no matter where you will be, we will always be your supporters. We will always be there for anything you need, even as we let go and give you the space to begin carving out a life on your own.

Perhaps the words ‘proud’ and ‘thankful’ are not really enough to express the feelings of the day. But I want to use them anyhow. I am so very proud not only of your significant academic achievements ( Being a topper in your class) but of the extraordinary human being that you have become. The fact you have topped your university is not a big thing for me. Because I already knew that you would graduate as a top student in your class and you destined to be successful in life. I feel incredibly lucky to call you, my sister.

And also I want to say sorry for not being a good brother for you, truth to be told, I've never considered myself a good brother for you or Homayoon, nor I considered myself a good son for mom, and in many ways, I've been simply horrible. I am sorry is about all I can offer.

Anyhow here is the deal, Marzia I will always be your big brother. And as such, I am allowed to give you some words of wisdom and insight. So, on your graduation day, here is the best advice I can offer you from my vantage point of being three years older:

Write your own story

You have only got this one life to live, forget anything happened up to this point; now, the rest of the book is empty. It is waiting for you to write it. How you fill the pages is up to you. As always, there will be successes, there will be failures, there will be heartbreaks, there will be happiness and significant accomplishments that make you pinch yourself as you wonder if they are real. There will be excitements, adventures, and goofs. But whatever you do, make sure the story is one you would want to read. Write your story and make it the greatest one ever told. Do not settle for anything less. Do not settle for less than extraordinary, you deserve the best, and when you are looking for it, it will find you.

The world is yours

You are finishing another chapter of your life and moving on. You have graduated from university, and you are off to accomplish great things. You can finally do what you want to do. You can follow your dreams and be what you wanted to be. From tomorrow, you are no longer a university student. You are part of this imaginary world, and it is all yours. You can make of it whatever you want. View the world as if the university was the best it can possibly get, and you will find the "real world" to be less exciting. (Those four years were incredible, yes, but you have a world of boundless opportunities ahead of you). I am sure you are going to do great things as you move forward with your life. You are special, and you are a unique combination of backgrounds, experiences, and passions. I am sure you are going to make a lasting impact on everyone that you meet. I have been there to see the amazing person you have become, and I cannot wait for the rest of the world to see that too. This is your chance, Marzia the world is yours. Get it!

Your big Brothoer, Hadi.
